# SECTION 4: EDUCATIONAL MODEL LEARNING

## Title: "Innovators in Education - Merging Skills with Academic Excellence"

The university represents innovators in merging skills with academic excellence through a pioneering skill-integrated higher education model contextualized for India.
## Educational Model & Learning Framework

### Vision of a Skills University  
**Horizontal & vertically integrated inclusive framework**

**Entry Pathways**
- 10th Pass → Level 2–3  
- ITI → Level 4  
- Apprentice → Level 4–5  
- NIOS linkage for dropouts  

**Progression within Skills University**
- Diploma → Level 5–6  
- Degree → Level 7–8  
- Masters → Level 9  

**Outcome Pathways**
- Doctorate in Automotive  
- Nano / Mini Entrepreneur  

**Key Features**
- Multiple entry & exit ramps and choice-based credit system allowing students to accumulate credits and earn qualifications at their own pace  
- Lifelong learning opportunities with RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) and work-integrated programs  


### Revamped Delivery Model at CUTM

**Year 1**
- **Semester 1 & 2**  
  Foundation Courses (STEM)  
  - Physics  
  - Chemistry  
  - Mathematics  
  - Engineering Drawing  

**Year 2**
- **Semester 3 & 4**  
  Core engineering courses; introduction to domains through skill electives  
  - Thermodynamics  
  - IC Engines  
  - Material Science  
  - Automotive Basics (two-wheeler, commercial vehicle, fuel systems)

**Year 3**
- **Semester 5 & 6**  
  Continued core engineering and domain-focused learning

**Year 4**
- **Semester 7 & 8**  
  Domain specialization with industry internship  
  - Automotive Design (Dassault System)  
  - Industrial Automation (Prolific Systems, Hyderabad)


### Institutional Model  
**Integration of Industry, Academia, Government & Social Entrepreneurship**

**Government**
- Policy, standards, and funding through various schemes and governance  
- Credibility and outreach for mobilization  

**Academia**
- Academic rigor through curriculum, trainers, and faculty  
- Assessments, certifications, and qualifications for higher education  

**Industry**
- Technical and knowledge support including equipment, curriculum, and training of trainers  
- On-the-job training (OJT) and employment opportunities  

**Social Entrepreneurship**
- Program implementation and mobilization  
- Industry connect, placements, and post-placement support

# SECTION 15: NSS CELL

## National Service Scheme (NSS) Program

**Authority:** Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, Government of India
**Establishment:** 1969 (Gandhiji's Centenary year)
**Scope:** Voluntary association for campus-community linkage

## NSS Motto
**"Not Me But You"**

**Philosophical Foundation:**
"The welfare of an individual is ultimately dependent on the welfare of the society on the whole, therefore, the NSS volunteers shall strive for the well-being of the society."

## 10 NSS Aims and Objectives

1. To understand the community in which they work

2. To understand themselves in relation to their community

3. To identify the needs and problems of the community and involve them in problem solving process

4. To develop among themselves a sense of social and civic responsibility

5. To utilise their knowledge in finding practical solution to individual and community problems

6. To develop competence required for group-living and sharing of responsibilities

7. To gain skills in mobilising community participation

8. To acquire leadership qualities and democratic attitude

9. To develop capacity to meet emergencies and natural disasters

10. To practice national integration and social harmony

## 14 NSS Activities

1. **Environment Enrichment and Conservation** - Environmental protection, sustainability
2. **Education and Recreation** - Educational support, learning facilitation
3. **Relief & Rehabilitation during Natural Calamities** - Disaster response
4. **Production Oriented Programmes** - Skill development, livelihood
5. **Social Service Programmes** - Community welfare
6. **Adoption of Slums** - Slum development
7. **Programmes for Women's Status Improvement** - Women empowerment
8. **Health, Family Welfare and Nutrition** - Health awareness
9. **Adoption of Villages** - Village development
10. **Coordination with Voluntary Organizations** - NGO partnerships
11. **Blood Donation** - Health service support
12. **Shramdaan** - Community manual labor
13. **Plantation** - Environmental conservation
14. **Immunisation** - Health awareness

## NSS Symbol

**Based On:** Konark Sun Temple's Rath Wheel (The Black Pagoda), Odisha

**Symbol Meaning:**
- **Wheel:** Portrays cycle of creation, preservation and release
- **Movement:** Signifies movement in life across time and space
- **Eight Bars:** Represent 24 hours of a day (24-hour service commitment)
- **Red Colour:** Young blood, lively, active, energetic, high spirit
- **Navy Blue Colour:** Cosmos, NSS as part of universal whole, welfare of mankind
