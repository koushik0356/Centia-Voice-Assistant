## Section 2: Research Policies and Guidelines

### Research Policy Overview

**URL:** https://cutmap.ac.in/research-policies-guidelines/

### Objectives of Research Policy

The broad objective of the research policy is to provide guidelines for undertaking high impact and context-specific applied research so that CUTM will be well-known in the chosen fields in the next five years. 

**Key Objectives Include:**
- Knowledge generation
- Evidence-based advocacy
- Experiential learning
- Strengthening the curriculum

### Organisation of Research Activities

Research activities at CUTM are organized in a **decentralized manner** with minimal central coordination for ensuring standardization of publication and high quality output.

#### Research Governance Structure

**1. Research Policy Group (RPG)**
- **Head:** Vice Chancellor
- **Member Secretary:** Director (Research)
- **Other Members:**
  - Pro-VC (Engineering)
  - Pro VC (Management)
  - Pro VC (Learning)
- **Frequency:** Meets twice a year
- **Responsibility:** Decide policy guidelines and themes for research at the University

**2. Research Advisory Group (RAG)**
- **Chair:** President (CUTM)
- **Member Secretary:** Director (Research)
- **Members:** President (CUTM), Vice-President (CUTM), experts from government, industry, civil society and academia
- **Frequency:** Meets once a year
- **Responsibility:** Advise the Research Council about the desired direction and scope of research at CUTM

**3. Research Council (RC)**
- **Chairperson:** Director (Research)
- **Members:**
  - Dean (Engineering)
  - Dean (Management)
  - Dean (TPC)
  - Director (Research)
- **Frequency:** Meets at least once in three months
- **Responsibility:** Facilitate research activities, monitor progress and ensure timely publication
- **Note:** Every department head or designated faculty has single point responsibility for all research-related activities of their department and reports to the Director (Research)

**4. Research Coordination Committee (RCC)**
- **Chairperson:** Director (Research)
- **Members:**
  - Director (Research)
  - Coordinator (Doctoral Research)
  - Coordinator (Graduate Research)
- **Frequency:** Meets once every month
- **Responsibility:** 
  - Research process standardization
  - Quality assurance
  - Progress monitoring
  - Publication coordination
  - Enhancing research capacity of faculty and students
  - Developing style manual
  - Facilitating linkage with outside institutions
  - Supporting in-house and outside publication
  - Creating enabling research environment through weekly seminar series and symposiums
  - Networking with researchers and institutions

### Organisation Structure of Research at CUTM

**Research at different levels:**

The Research Coordination Committee ensures that all research activities are standardized and reasonable quality is assured.

### Research Publication

**Faculty Publications:**
- Expected to publish at least one Working Paper per year
- Output published as:
  - In-house Working Papers
  - Research Studies
  - Refereed journals (encouraged)

**Student Publications:**
- Output published as:
  - Dissertations
  - Theses
  - Research reports

**Quality Assurance Process for Working Papers:**
1. Submission to a committee (to ensure quality)
2. Internal referee review and assessment of:
   - Theoretical contribution
   - Empirical contribution
3. Requirement for literature review and inclusion of new material
4. Prescribed referencing pattern must be followed
5. Grading of Working Papers:
   - i) Publishable
   - ii) Publishable with revision
   - iii) Not publishable
6. Referee reports should be available within 3 weeks
7. The working paper can be put on hold if it affects publication in a journal

**Language Support:** Language editing facility available to faculty and students

### Incentives for Research

Incentives in the form of financial reward and promotion are an integral part of the research policy of the University. Specific incentives will be announced from time to time.
