The

**B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering (Biomedical)** is a **four-year undergraduate program** that combines the principles of **electronics, communication, and biomedical sciences** to develop advanced healthcare technologies. This program focuses on **medical instrumentation, biosignal processing, medical imaging, wearable health devices, and AI-based healthcare innovations**.

With the growing integration of

**technology in healthcare**, this field offers **exciting career opportunities** in **medical device manufacturing, hospital technology management, biomedical research, and AI-driven healthcare solutions**.

**Key Highlights of B.Tech in ECE (Biomedical)**

**Duration:** 4 years (8 semesters)

**Mode of Study:** Full-time, **practical and industry-integrated learning**

**Key Subjects Covered:** **Biomedical Electronics & Instrumentation**

- Medical Sensors & Wearable Devices

- Bioelectronic Circuits & Signal Processing

- Microcontrollers & Embedded Systems for Healthcare

**Medical Imaging & Signal Processing**

- MRI, CT, and Ultrasound Technologies

- AI in Medical Imaging & Diagnosis

- Biosignal Processing (ECG, EEG, EMG)

**Healthcare Technology & AI Applications**

- IoT & AI in Smart Healthcare

- Robotics in Surgery & Rehabilitation

- Wireless Communication for Telemedicine

**Hands-on Labs & Practical Exposure:** **Biomedical Sensors & IoT-based Health Monitoring** **Medical Imaging & AI-based Diagnosis Labs** **Signal Processing & Wearable Device Development** **Robotics & Automation in Healthcare**

**Industry Certifications & Tools Used:** **Medical Signal Processing:**MATLAB, Python, LabVIEW **Embedded Systems for Healthcare:**Keil, ARM, Raspberry Pi **AI & Machine Learning in Healthcare:**TensorFlow, PyTorch **Medical Imaging Technologies:**DICOM, OpenCV

**Why Choose B.Tech in ECE (Biomedical)?**

**Interdisciplinary Field** – Combines **electronics, communication, and medical technology**.

**High Industry Demand** – Growing need for **healthcare technology, medical devices, and AI-powered diagnostics**.

**Emerging Technologies** – Work with **IoT in healthcare, medical robotics, and AI in diagnostics**.

**Global Career Opportunities** – Biomedical engineers are in demand worldwide in **hospitals, medical device industries, research labs, and healthcare startups**.
