The **B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering** is a **four-year undergraduate program** that focuses on the **design, development, analysis, and manufacturing of mechanical systems**. This program provides students with **strong theoretical and practical knowledge** in areas such as **thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, robotics, CAD/CAM, automation, and renewable energy technologies**.

Mechanical Engineering is one of the **broadest and most versatile branches of engineering**, offering opportunities in **automotive, aerospace, energy, robotics, manufacturing, and artificial intelligence-driven automation** industries.

**Key Highlights of B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering**

- **Duration:** 4 years (8 semesters)
- **Mode of Study:** Full-time, **industry-integrated practical learning**

**Key Subjects Covered: Core Mechanical Engineering Concepts**

- Engineering Mechanics & Strength of Materials
- Fluid Mechanics & Heat Transfer
- Thermodynamics & Power Plant Engineering

**Design & Manufacturing**

- Computer-Aided Design (CAD) & Manufacturing (CAM)
- Robotics & Automation in Manufacturing
- Additive Manufacturing (3D Printing)

**Emerging Technologies in Mechanical Engineering**

- AI & Machine Learning in Mechanical Systems
- Internet of Things (IoT) in Smart Manufacturing
- Renewable Energy & Sustainability

**Hands-on Labs & Practical Exposure:**

- CNC Machining & CAD/CAM Software (AutoCAD, SolidWorks, ANSYS)
- Robotics & Automation Labs
- 3D Printing & Prototyping Labs
- Thermal & Fluid Dynamics Simulations

**Industry Certifications & Tools Used:**

- **Design & Analysis:** AutoCAD, SolidWorks, CATIA, ANSYS
- **Manufacturing & Automation:** Siemens NX, Fusion 360
- **Thermal & Fluid Analysis:** MATLAB, CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics)

**Why Choose B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering?**

- **Versatile Career Options** – Opportunities in **automotive, aerospace, robotics, manufacturing, AI-driven automation, and renewable energy sectors**.
- **High Industry Demand** – Mechanical engineers are essential for **modern industrial automation, smart manufacturing, and sustainable energy solutions**.
- **Emerging Technologies** – Learn **3D printing, AI in mechanical systems, robotics, and green energy innovations**.
- **Global Career Opportunities** – Work in **top industries, research institutions, and international engineering firms**.

### Career Path & Job Roles

Graduates of **B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering** can pursue careers in:

**1. Automotive & Aerospace Engineering**

- Automobile Engineer  
- Aerospace Engineer  
- Vehicle Dynamics Engineer

**2. Robotics & Automation**

- Robotics Engineer  
- Industrial Automation Specialist  
- AI-Driven Manufacturing Engineer

**3. Thermal & Energy Systems**

- Power Plant Engineer  
- Renewable Energy Engineer (Solar, Wind, Hydro)  
- HVAC Engineer

**4. Product Design & Manufacturing**

- CAD/CAM Engineer  
- 3D Printing & Prototyping Engineer  
- Quality Control & Production Engineer

**5. Research & Development**

- Mechanical Research Scientist  
- Materials & Nanotechnology Engineer  
- Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) Specialist
